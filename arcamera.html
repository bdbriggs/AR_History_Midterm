Harrison County Courthouse

1901

Move closer and aim your phone toward the site to reveal the history card.

The historic Harrison County Courthouse in Marshall, Texas, was designed by architect J. Riely Gordon and completed in 1901. This Beaux Arts / Neo-Classical landmark is known for its pink granite base, central dome, and the statue of Lady Justice that crowns the structure.

After a new courthouse was built in 1964, the 1901 building was restored around 2009–2010 and transformed into the Harrison County Historical Museum, preserving local history inside one of Harrison County’s most recognizable buildings.
