1901

1901 Courthouse History

Designed by architect J. Riely Gordon and completed in 1901, this Beaux Arts / Neo-Classical landmark features a pink granite base, central dome, and the statue of Lady Justice. It is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

After a new courthouse was built in 1964, the 1901 building was restored (2009–2010) and transformed into the Harrison County Historical Museum — preserving local history while still housing some government offices.

Through the Years

🏛️

1901

Courthouse Opens

Designed by J. Riely Gordon in Beaux Arts style, the courthouse opens with pink granite facades and a gilded dome. Lady Justice watches over Marshall from above.

⚖️

1964

New Courthouse Built

County government moves to a modern facility. The 1901 landmark sits largely vacant for decades — its future uncertain, but its architecture too significant to abandon.

🔧

2009–2010

Grand Restoration

A multi-year restoration returns the building to its former glory. Original details are preserved, the dome is reinforced, and the building is added to the National Register of Historic Places.

🗂️

Today

Harrison County Historical Museum

Part archive, part museum, part living civic space. Visitors explore East Texas history in the same halls where justice was once served.
