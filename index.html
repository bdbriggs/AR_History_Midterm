1901 Courthouse History

The historic Harrison County Courthouse in Marshall, Texas, was designed by architect J. Riely Gordon and completed in 1901. This Beaux Arts / Neo-Classical landmark is known for its pink granite base, central dome, and the statue of Lady Justice that crowns the structure. It stands as the county’s fourth courthouse and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

After a new courthouse was built in 1964, the 1901 building was later restored (2009–2010) and transformed into the Harrison County Historical Museum. Today, it preserves local history while still housing some government offices, blending past and present in one iconic structure.
